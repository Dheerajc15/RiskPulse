Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) — Key Releases 2024

10-Year Treasury Yield (DGS10):
- January 2024: 3.95%, trending upward from Q4 2023 lows
- The yield curve remained inverted (2Y-10Y spread negative) for 18+ consecutive months
- Implication: Persistent inversion signals recession risk, though equities continued rallying

Unemployment Rate (UNRATE):
- January 2024: 3.7%, near 50-year lows
- Labor market tightness supports consumer spending but constrains Fed rate cuts
- Implication: Higher-for-longer rate environment, negative for duration-sensitive assets

Consumer Price Index (CPIAUCSL):
- YoY inflation: 3.1% in January 2024, down from 6.4% peak in January 2023
- Core CPI (excluding food/energy): 3.9%, still above Fed's 2% target
- Implication: Disinflation trend intact but last mile to 2% will be bumpy

Federal Funds Rate (FEDFUNDS):
- Held at 5.25-5.50% since July 2023 (16-year high)
- Market pricing 3-4 cuts in 2024 (CME FedWatch as of Jan 2024)
- Implication: Rate-sensitive sectors (REITs, utilities) positioned for outperformance on cuts

VIX Index (VIXCLS):
- Average Q4 2023: 14.2, well below long-term average of 19.5
- Low VIX paradox: compressed vol often precedes sharp regime changes
- GARCH analysis shows volatility clustering — current calm likely temporary
